The Statewide Innovation Leadership Network

A statewide community for leaders committed to redesigning public education.

Every leader who participates in any Institute experience automatically becomes part of something larger: a network of North Carolina educators committed to rethinking, reimagining, and redesigning public education.

Why the Network exists

The connective tissue that turns individual efforts into a statewide movement.

Leading innovation in a public school system can be isolating work. The pressures are relentless, the politics are real, and the leaders most committed to change are often the ones who feel most alone in it. The Network exists to change that.

North Carolina already has something rare: a critical mass of district leaders who believe the status quo is not good enough and who are actively working to do something about it. What has been missing is the shared language, trusted relationships, and common infrastructure that turns that energy into something bigger.

It is not a mailing list or a conference circuit. It is an ongoing, active community of leaders who share challenges, exchange resources, surface emerging practice, and hold one another accountable to the work they have committed to do.

What the Network offers

Practical connection, shared learning, and stronger implementation infrastructure.

Peer connection and problem-solving

Members have direct access to peers across North Carolina who are navigating similar challenges, from governance alignment to piloting culture to redesign strategy.

Shared learning and emerging practice

As districts pursue design journeys, the Network surfaces what is working, what is not, and what is worth adapting so members do not have to start from scratch.

Curated resources and model examples

Members get access to vetted, contextualized tools, frameworks, case studies, and model examples drawn from North Carolina and beyond.

Topical working groups

On issues such as AI integration, high school redesign, community engagement, and budget flexibility, the Network convenes focused groups that go deeper than any single course or convening can.

Annual statewide convening

Each year, the full Network gathers for a signature working session that celebrates progress, surfaces new ideas, and renews the collective commitment to transformation.

The Curated Marketplace

A vetted way to find the right support when districts need specialized expertise.

One of the Network's most practical features is a Curated Marketplace of vetted partners and specialists available to districts navigating specific challenges. The Institute's core programming is deep but not exhaustive. There are areas such as leadership coaching, political communication, school design, board development, strategic planning, data systems, and community engagement where districts need expert support beyond what the Institute itself provides.

Rather than leaving leaders to sort through an uneven landscape of consultants and vendors on their own, the Network maintains a carefully curated roster of trusted specialists and organizations. Every partner in the Marketplace is vetted for quality, alignment with learner-centered values, and demonstrated effectiveness in public school contexts.

When a district identifies a need, the Network can connect them quickly to the right support, preventing duplication of effort, building on what already exists in the field, and ensuring that districts are not starting from zero every time they face a new challenge.

Network members can access the Marketplace through their Institute member portal. Any Institute course, convening, fellowship, or study tour enrolls you automatically.

Built on North Carolina's existing strengths

Extending a strong foundation rather than starting from scratch.

This Network builds on and strengthens The Innovation Project's already vibrant network of North Carolina district leaders, which has spent years cultivating the trusted relationships and collaborative culture that make this kind of community possible.

The Institute's statewide network is designed to extend that foundation, connecting more leaders across more roles and more districts, and giving the existing TIP community a richer set of shared experiences and resources to draw from.

It also directly advances North Carolina's own strategic vision, which calls explicitly for statewide and regional education networks that support implementation, share scalable resources, and accelerate the state's most important priorities.
