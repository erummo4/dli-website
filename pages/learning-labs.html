Leading Innovation in Public Education

Duration: 4 weeks

Format: Hybrid (2 in-person days + weekly virtual sessions)

This foundations course is the natural entry point for leaders who are new to the Institute or to structured innovation work. It introduces the core frameworks that undergird the Institute's approach: how to assess a district's conditions for change, how to engage communities in design, and how to think about the relationship between strategy and redesign. Participants examine real North Carolina examples alongside national models, and work through a structured reflection on their own district's readiness and priorities.

By the end of the course, participants will have completed a preliminary innovation readiness diagnostic for their district and drafted an initial "conditions for change" memo they can share with their cabinet or board. The course also serves as an on-ramp to more specialized offerings, helping participants see which Institute pathways are most relevant to their next phase of work.

Who it's for: Superintendents and senior district leaders new to redesign work; also appropriate for board chairs seeking a shared framework with their superintendent

What you'll get: Innovation Readiness Diagnostic, Conditions for Change memo, peer cohort connections

Cost: $1,200 per participant

Credential: Certificate in Education Innovation Foundations, NC Education Innovation Leadership Institute

Faculty: Lead facilitators from Transcend's leadership development team, co-facilitated with a veteran North Carolina superintendent from The Innovation Project network

Community-Based Design for District Leaders

Duration: 6 weeks

Format: Hybrid (1 in-person kickoff day + weekly virtual sessions)

Meaningful redesign does not happen when leaders plan in isolation and then announce to communities. It happens when students, families, educators, and community members are genuinely involved in shaping what learning looks like. This course builds the practical skills district leaders need to run authentic community-based design processes, from structuring listening sessions and stakeholder engagements to synthesizing community input into actionable design principles that actually influence decisions.

Participants will practice facilitation techniques, work through common pitfalls, and build a community engagement plan tailored to a real initiative in their district. Guest practitioners who have led community-based design in North Carolina contexts will join several sessions to share what worked, what did not, and what they wish they had known. By the end of the course, participants will have a complete community engagement roadmap for an actual project in their district.

Who it's for: Superintendents, chief academic officers, chief innovation officers, and district leaders responsible for strategic planning or school redesign

What you'll get: Community Engagement Roadmap, facilitation toolkit, access to Transcend's community-based design resources

Cost: $1,400 per participant

Credential: Certificate in Community-Based Design, NC Education Innovation Leadership Institute

Faculty: Transcend community design practitioners; guest practitioners from NC districts with demonstrated community engagement experience

Governing for Innovation: A Course for Board Leaders

Duration: 5 days (delivered as a 2-day retreat + 3 individual coaching sessions over 6 weeks)

Format: In-person retreat + virtual coaching

School boards are often the unsung variable in whether innovation succeeds or stalls. A board that does not share a superintendent's vision, or does not understand what redesign actually requires, can slow or stop meaningful change even when leaders are doing everything right. This course is designed specifically for board members and board chairs who want to govern more effectively during a period of intentional transformation.

Participants will examine real case studies of board-superintendent alignment and misalignment, work through governance frameworks designed for innovation contexts, and develop concrete agreements about how their board will engage with redesign efforts over the next year. The three follow-up coaching sessions allow each board to apply what they have learned to their specific governance challenges and decisions. This course is most powerful when a superintendent and board chair attend together.

Who it's for: Board members, board chairs, ideally attending alongside their superintendent

What you'll get: Governance for Innovation framework, board-superintendent alignment agreement template, 3 individual coaching sessions

Cost: $2,200 per board team of up to 4 (additional members $400 each)

Credential: Certificate in Innovation Governance, NC Education Innovation Leadership Institute

Faculty: Former NC school board members with innovation leadership experience; Transcend senior advisors; The Innovation Project leadership

AI as a Leadership and Learning Tool

Duration: 3 weeks

Format: Fully virtual (live sessions + asynchronous work)

Artificial intelligence is already reshaping classrooms, administrative functions, and public expectations of schools, and most district leaders feel underprepared to lead through it. This course moves past introductory AI literacy and focuses on what leaders actually need: a framework for making responsible decisions about AI adoption, a grounded understanding of how AI affects teaching and learning, and practical tools for communicating about AI with boards, families, and staff.

Participants engage with real AI tools throughout the course, not just to understand them theoretically, but to practice using them as part of their own leadership and problem-solving. By the end, participants will have drafted an AI readiness framework for their district and a communication guide for their community.

Who it's for: Superintendents, chief academic officers, chief innovation officers, and technology directors; open to individuals and district teams

What you'll get: AI Readiness Framework, district communication guide, curated resource library

Cost: $850 per participant

Credential: Certificate in AI Leadership for Education, NC Education Innovation Leadership Institute

Faculty: Transcend AI and learning design experts; guest practitioners from districts leading responsible AI integration

Building Budget Space for Innovation

Duration: 5 days (delivered as two 2-day workshops, 3 weeks apart, plus one follow-up virtual session)

Format: In-person workshops + virtual

One of the most common barriers to sustained innovation is not will, it is money. Districts that want to invest in redesign often struggle to find flexibility within existing budgets, funding streams, and board expectations. This course, designed specifically for district financial leaders, addresses that challenge directly.

Participants learn how to audit current spending for innovation alignment, identify underutilized funding streams, build the financial case for innovation investments, and structure budgets that can absorb the startup costs of redesign without destabilizing core operations. Each participant brings their own district's budget data and works through a structured analysis over the two workshops, leaving with an actionable recommendations memo they can present to their superintendent and board.

Who it's for: Chief financial officers, deputy superintendents for finance, and district business officers; ideally attending alongside their superintendent or CAO

What you'll get: Budget audit framework, innovation funding analysis for your district, recommendations memo template

Cost: $1,600 per participant

Credential: Certificate in Innovation Finance for District Leaders, NC Education Innovation Leadership Institute

Faculty: NC school finance veterans; national experts in education funding flexibility; The Innovation Project network practitioners

Piloting and Evidence-Building in Schools and Districts

Duration: 8 weeks

Format: Hybrid (1 in-person day + biweekly virtual sessions)

Most districts have no shortage of new ideas being tried. What they often lack is a rigorous, practical process for testing those ideas, learning from what happens, and deciding what to scale, adapt, or stop. This course teaches district leaders how to design and run meaningful pilots, not performative ones.

Participants learn how to frame a clear question, identify appropriate evidence, design a manageable test, and build the internal culture that treats honest failure as useful information rather than political liability. By the end, participants will have a complete pilot design document for a real district initiative, including a logic model, an evidence plan, and a decision framework for what happens after the pilot concludes.

Who it's for: Chief academic officers, chief innovation officers, directors of strategy or research; open to district teams

What you'll get: Pilot design document, evidence planning toolkit, decision framework template

Cost: $1,500 per participant

Credential: Certificate in Innovation Piloting and Evidence Practice, NC Education Innovation Leadership Institute

Faculty: Research and design practitioners from Transcend; guest faculty from NC universities with applied education research expertise

High School Redesign: From Vision to Design

Duration: 10 weeks

Format: Hybrid (2 in-person days + biweekly virtual sessions)

High school is the arena where the gap between what students need and what schools currently offer is most stark, and where redesign is most complex, most political, and most consequential. This course is built for district teams who are ready to move beyond conversations about the future of high school and into the actual work of redesigning it.

Participants examine the structural and cultural assumptions built into the current high school model, study compelling redesign examples from North Carolina and nationally, and use a structured design process to develop a high school redesign vision and action plan for their own community. Each team is expected to conduct at least two structured listening sessions with students during the course and bring that data back to the cohort for reflection and peer challenge.

Who it's for: District teams of 3-5 including superintendent, CAO, and high school principal or innovation director

What you'll get: High School Redesign Vision document, community listening report, action plan with 90-day next steps

Cost: $3,500 per district team

Credential: Certificate in High School Redesign Leadership, NC Education Innovation Leadership Institute

Faculty: Transcend school design experts; NC high school redesign practitioners; guest faculty with expertise in career-connected learning and project-based learning

Designing Districts as Learning Organizations

Duration: 6 weeks

Format: Fully virtual (weekly live sessions + asynchronous work)

Most districts ask teachers to build learning cultures in classrooms while the district itself operates as a compliance-and-delivery machine. This course invites senior district leaders to examine that gap and begin to close it by redesigning how their district learns, adapts, and improves at the organizational level.

Drawing on organizational learning theory, knowledge management research, and practical examples from school systems that have successfully built learning cultures, participants work through what it would mean for their district to become genuinely curious, evidence-responsive, and capable of improving in real time. The final project asks each participant to design a learning organization initiative for their district.

Who it's for: Superintendents, CAOs, chief innovation officers, directors of curriculum and instruction; best taken as a district team of 2-3

What you'll get: Organizational learning diagnostic, learning organization initiative design, curated resource library

Cost: $1,100 per participant

Credential: Certificate in Organizational Learning for District Leaders, NC Education Innovation Leadership Institute

Faculty: Transcend learning design practitioners; guest faculty from education management and organizational development fields

Strategic Communications for Education Innovation

Duration: 5 days (delivered as a 3-day intensive workshop + 2 virtual follow-up sessions over 4 weeks)

Format: In-person intensive + virtual

Innovation fails in public systems for many reasons, and one of the most preventable is poor communication. When leaders cannot clearly explain what they are doing, why it matters, and what families and communities should expect, public confidence erodes, board support softens, and staff cynicism grows.

This course is designed for the district leaders responsible for building public understanding and support for change. Participants will develop a complete communications strategy for a current or planned innovation initiative in their district, from core narrative and audience mapping to specific messages, channels, and an engagement calendar. They leave not just with a plan, but with practiced confidence in delivering their message.

Who it's for: Superintendents, chief communications officers, and senior district leaders with communications responsibilities; board chairs are also encouraged to attend

What you'll get: Innovation communications strategy, stakeholder messaging guide, media engagement toolkit

Cost: $1,800 per participant

Credential: Certificate in Strategic Communications for Education Innovation, NC Education Innovation Leadership Institute

Faculty: Experienced education communications strategists; former NC district communications leaders; The Innovation Project network practitioners
