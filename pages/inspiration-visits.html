Inspiration Visits

Study tours built around a clear learning purpose, not just a site visit.

One of the biggest barriers to meaningful change is not lack of goodwill, it is a lack of vivid, credible exposure to what else is possible. These visits bring North Carolina leaders face-to-face with compelling examples of redesigned learning in the country and the world.

Back to home Next: Network

Participants prepare before they arrive, observe with discipline while they are there, and reflect afterward on what is transferable, what is not, and what the experience means for their own communities.

When superintendents, cabinet members, and board leaders make these visits together, they often return home better aligned around a shared vision than they have ever been.

Reinventing the Rural School: Lessons from Northern Cass

Location: Hunter, North Dakota

Length: 1 day

Date: April 17, 2026

Northern Cass School District is one of the most cited examples of a small, rural district that undertook a genuine, community-rooted transformation of what school looks like and made it stick. Under Superintendent Cory Steiner, Northern Cass moved away from a traditional seat-time model toward a competency-based system built around a clear graduate profile and a deep commitment to student agency.

Participants will spend the day inside Northern Cass schools, meeting with Superintendent Steiner and his leadership team, observing classrooms, and engaging in structured reflection on what the Northern Cass model means for their own context. The day is organized around three questions: What did they change, and why? What did it take to lead that change? And what would we need to do differently at home to pursue something similar?

Who it's for: Superintendents and senior district leaders, particularly from rural and small-to-mid-sized NC districts; board chairs welcome

Group size: Maximum 18 participants

Price: $950 per participant (includes facilitation, materials, and meals; travel and lodging not included)

Credential: Study Tour Certificate of Participation, NC Education Innovation Leadership Institute

Location: San Diego, California

Length: 2 days

Date: May 7-8, 2026

High Tech High is one of the most studied and visited school communities in the world. Location: Washington, D.C.

How to Prepare for a Study Tour

Three components distinguish every Institute tour from an ordinary site visit.

Before you go

Participants receive a preparation guide with background reading on the host community, observation lenses tailored to the tour's learning focus, and a short reflection prompt to help them arrive with clear questions in mind.

While you're there

Institute facilitators structure each day around specific learning goals, not just open observation. Participants use shared observation protocols and have dedicated time to compare notes and sharpen their thinking with peers.

After you return

Within two weeks of the tour, participants join a 90-minute virtual debrief session with their cohort to surface what they learned, what they are questioning, and what they want to do next.

All study tour participants automatically join the NC Education Innovation Leadership Institute's statewide network.

Group discounts are available for districts sending teams of 3 or more to the same tour.
