Fellowships

The Institute's most immersive offering for sustained, courageous leadership.

Designed not just to build knowledge, but to strengthen the personal and adaptive leadership capacity that makes meaningful change possible in public systems.

Back to home Next: Inspiration Visits

Fellowships are role-alike by design. Superintendents work alongside superintendents. Cabinet leaders work alongside cabinet leaders. Board chairs work alongside board chairs. This structure creates the depth of honesty and trust that mixed-role programs rarely achieve and produces the kind of peer relationships that carry leaders through the hardest moments of redesign.

There are currently three fellowship tracks. An In-District Fellowship option is also available for districts whose leaders have already participated in a state-level fellowship.

The Superintendent Fellowship

Duration: 9 months

Format: Opening retreat + monthly virtual cohort sessions + 3 individual coaching sessions

Leading systemic change in a public school district is one of the most demanding leadership challenges there is. Superintendents face isolation, political resistance, board pressure, media scrutiny, and fatigue, all while staying connected to purpose and managing relentless operational demands. Most leadership development addresses the technical and strategic dimensions of this work. This fellowship addresses something equally important and far less often discussed: the personal, adaptive leadership capacity required to lead meaningful change over time without burning out or backing down.

The fellowship is grounded in the framework of Learner-Centered Leadership, developed collaboratively with superintendents across the country through Transcend's national Learner-Centered Leadership Lab. Fellows examine their own leadership core, including values, identity, and motivations, and develop the mindsets and competencies that translate that core into conditions for innovation in their districts.

The opening retreat builds the foundation of candor and trust that the rest of the fellowship depends on. Monthly cohort sessions maintain accountability, peer challenge, and connection. Three individual coaching sessions give each fellow space to work through the specific leadership challenges they are navigating in real time. Cohorts are kept deliberately small because the fellowship's value depends entirely on the depth of honesty participants bring to it.

Who it's for: Superintendents, particularly those leading or preparing to lead significant redesign efforts

What you'll get: Personal leadership reflection journal, peer accountability partnership, individual coaching sessions, and ongoing cohort community

Cost: $4,500 per participant

Credential: Certificate in Learner-Centered Leadership, NC Education Innovation Leadership Institute; eligible for 40 hours of NC administrator continuing education credit (pending approval)

Faculty: Senior Transcend leadership coaches; The Innovation Project executive leadership; guest faculty from Transcend's national Learner-Centered Leadership Lab

The Senior Cabinet Fellowship

Duration: 9 months

Format: Opening retreat + monthly virtual cohort sessions + 2 individual coaching sessions

Chief academic officers, chief innovation officers, deputies, and other senior district leaders are often the people doing the most intense day-to-day work of redesign, translating a superintendent's vision into actual changes in schools, managing resistance from staff, and holding multiple complex initiatives at once. And yet they rarely have a dedicated space to develop their own leadership alongside peers who truly understand the demands of the role.

This fellowship brings together senior cabinet leaders from across North Carolina in a structured, sustained cohort experience. Fellows explore what it means to lead from the middle of a system, building conviction and coalition upward, downward, and across, and develop practical frameworks for community-based design, piloting, and the conditions required for innovation to stick.

Drawing on the same Learner-Centered Leadership framework that anchors the Superintendent Fellowship, this experience helps cabinet leaders examine how their own core identity and concept of their role shapes their impact, and how to deepen both.

Who it's for: Chief academic officers, chief innovation officers, deputy superintendents, and senior district leaders responsible for instructional or innovation strategy

What you'll get: Learner-Centered Leadership diagnostic, peer cohort connections, 2 individual coaching sessions

Cost: $3,500 per participant

Credential: Certificate in Learner-Centered Leadership for Senior District Leaders, NC Education Innovation Leadership Institute

Faculty: Transcend leadership development practitioners; The Innovation Project network leaders; guest practitioners from NC districts

The Board Chair Fellowship

Duration: 6 months

Format: Opening retreat + bimonthly virtual cohort sessions + 1 individual coaching session

School boards are often the least-developed variable in whether innovation succeeds or stalls. A board chair who does not share a superintendent's vision, or who does not understand what meaningful redesign actually requires of governance, can slow or stop progress even when district leaders are doing everything right. This fellowship is built specifically for board chairs who want to govern more effectively during a period of intentional transformation.

Fellows explore the distinct leadership role of a board chair in innovation contexts: how to create the conditions for bold decision-making, hold accountability and creative risk-taking at the same time, and build alignment within the full board around a coherent vision for change. Cohort sessions include structured case work, peer exchange, and facilitated reflection on real governance challenges fellows are navigating.

Who it's for: Board chairs; most valuable when the district's superintendent is simultaneously participating in the Superintendent Fellowship

What you'll get: Governance for Innovation framework, peer cohort connections, 1 individual coaching session

Cost: $2,500 per participant

Credential: Certificate in Innovation Governance Leadership, NC Education Innovation Leadership Institute

Faculty: Former NC board chairs with innovation leadership experience; Transcend senior advisors; The Innovation Project leadership

In-District Fellowships

Extend learner-centered leadership beyond the state-level cohorts.

Once a district's leadership team has participated in one or more of the state-level fellowships, the Institute can bring a customized fellowship experience directly into that district. In-District Fellowships extend the learner-centered leadership framework to principals, teacher leaders, counselors, and others in informal leadership roles, building a coherent, shared leadership culture across the entire system rather than just at the top.

This model is inspired by the work of Lindsay Unified School District in California, where Superintendent Tom Rooney and his team partnered with Transcend to bring the Learner-Centered Leadership Lab to every administrator and principal in the district, and then to teacher leaders and others throughout the system. The result was a district-wide culture of leadership grounded in shared values, trust, and a collective commitment to learners.

In-District Fellowships are designed in close partnership with each district's leadership and are scoped to fit local context, priorities, and capacity.

All fellows automatically become part of the NC Education Innovation Leadership Institute's statewide innovation leadership network.
