October 14-15, 2025

North Carolina Innovation Accelerator

The Umstead Hotel & Spa, Cary, NC

This is the Institute's signature launch convening, a two-day working experience for superintendents, selected cabinet leaders, and board members ready to engage seriously with the forces reshaping public education. Participants will examine what is changing in education, reflect on what North Carolina's 2030 priorities mean in their specific local context, and honestly assess their district's current conditions for change.

This is not a sit-and-listen event. Leaders come ready to work, and leave with a clearer diagnosis of where their system is ready to move and where it is stuck. By the end of the two days, participants will walk away with a sharper picture of their district's innovation readiness, practical frameworks for engaging their communities in redesign, and a peer cohort of North Carolina leaders facing the same pressures. Light receptions both evenings create space for the relationship-building that makes the work sustainable.

Who can attend: Superintendents and up to 2 district/board leaders per district

Cost: $750 per participant (includes meals and materials; lodging not included)

January 22, 2026

AI and the Future of Learning: A Leadership Workshop

Friday Institute for Educational Innovation, Raleigh, NC

This one-day topical convening is designed for superintendents and senior district leaders who want to move past the hype and get practical about artificial intelligence, both as a leadership tool and as a student learning challenge. Participants will explore how AI is already reshaping classrooms, workforce expectations, and community concerns, and will work through real scenarios and decision frameworks together.

The day is structured around active problem-solving with facilitators from both The Innovation Project and Transcend guiding teams through applied exercises. Participants will leave a clearer sense of their own vision for learning with AI, guidance for piloting new approaches and how to communicate about AI with boards and families, and new connections with peers across the state who are navigating the same terrain. Districts are encouraged, though not required, to bring a small team to maximize what they take home.

Who can attend: Superintendents, CAOs, chief innovation officers, and senior district leaders

Cost: $350 per participant (includes meals and materials)

March 4-5, 2026

Redesigning High School: A Working Convening for District Teams

The Proximity Hotel, Greensboro, NC

High school is where urgency and complexity collide, and where the gap between what students need and what most systems currently offer is most visible. This two-day convening invites district teams to examine the assumptions built into the traditional high school model, learn from emerging redesign examples from across North Carolina and beyond, and begin mapping what a more relevant and future-ready high school experience could look like in their community.

Facilitated by Transcend's school design experts alongside North Carolina practitioners, sessions blend big-picture challenge with grounded, local application. Teams will leave with a shared vocabulary for redesign, an initial diagnostic of their high school's strengths and constraints, and a set of next steps they can actually act on. This convening is intentionally designed for teams because sustainable high school redesign requires alignment among the superintendent, board leadership, and school-level leaders from the start. Dinner on the first evening is included and will feature a facilitated conversation with a North Carolina superintendent who is already leading this work.

Who can attend: District teams of 3-5 (superintendent + board and/or school leaders); limited to 12 teams statewide

Cost: $1,200 per team (includes all meals and materials; lodging not included)

All convenings are part of the NC Education Innovation Leadership Institute, co-stewarded by The Innovation Project and Transcend.

Participants in any convening are invited to join the statewide innovation leadership network.
